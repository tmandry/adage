Adage – Project Definition


Story:

You're an experienced hacker, and you're up for hire. The groups that hire you want you to help them to reach a specific destination, like a vault in a building, an enemy, etc. In order to accomplish their goals. Your job is to gain access to any resources that may help you, including security cameras, phone logs, etc. and report to your employers, as well as use your hacking abilities to guide them through security traps and checkpoints. You are an intelligence network of one, and your job is not easy. Fortunately it pays well, which allows you to buy new equipment and software. You start off with very little, in money and experience, and so you are forced to do easy jobs at first. But, as you gain experience, the jobs will become more and more complex, and pay more as well. Be careful, though, if you're clumsy, you have a worse chance of getting hired, and you are putting your life on the line.


Interface:

  • Blueprint: There will be a blueprint window with whatever information you can salvage. Locations on the blueprint where you can see what is going on (camera) are highlighted, while other locations are darkened (but you can still see the map). Different symbols will represent things like people, cameras, doors, locks, and other things. Actions of people (ex.: a guard eating a donut, not paying attention) may be shown bubbles over the blueprint.

  • Status: This window will show important information, as well as dialouge between you and the team. If you fail to warn the team of an upcoming guard, or they are forced to fight, you will know immediately, and you will also know of any injuries or deaths of all people (including guards).

  • Terminal: This is where you execute all commands, and do your hacking and such from. You can, of course, open multiple terminal sessions. This could be much like a linux terminal or something. Needs ideas.

  • How you're doing: This window displays things like your money, and jobs that have been offered to you. Not much use while doing a mission.

  • Store: Where you buy stuff from, including software and hardware.


Process:

  1. You accept a job, knowing the difficulty and a general idea of what you're doing, as well as the money you're getting

  2. If you agree on a small advance payment, you are awarded that and given an option to buy hardware/software before you start

  3. You are briefed on the mission, who the people are, amount of security in the building etc.

  4. You are taken to the mission interface, where you start your mission.

  5. Throughout the mission you are given more details at each stage. The team does not know everything either, new things can pop up at any time.

  6. If the mission is a success, you are rewarded their base payment, plus bonuses, minus penalties. Don't forget bragging rights.

  7. If the mission is a failure, you are not rewarded anything, except for that you get to keep the advance payment, if any.

  8. You start the process over again! \~:-D
